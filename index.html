The Regulatory Backdrop

H6 & FCAC: What They Require, and Where Most Institutions Fall Short

The requirements aren't ambiguous — but most institutions are still running biller onboarding on spreadsheets and handling complaints in disconnected systems. That gap is the compliance risk.

📋

Biller Onboarding Has No Audit Trail

H6 requires documented third-party risk management for every biller. Email threads and spreadsheets aren't auditable, aren't version-controlled, and won't satisfy an examiner.

⏱

SLAs Are Absent Across the Board

From biller onboarding to complaint resolution, most institutions have no systematic SLA framework in place. Onboarding timelines drift without accountability. Complaint resolution depends on individual effort rather than governed process. Both expose the institution to H6 and FCAC scrutiny.

🗃

Biller Data Is Fragmented and Inaccurate

Duplicate records, stale data, and siloed biller information create downstream problems — payment errors, customer complaints, and an inability to surface a clean registry to customers or regulators.
